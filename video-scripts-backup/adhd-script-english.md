# **English Version (Same Voice, Not Literal Translation)**

## [Your Brain]

Hey.

Parts of my brain don’t work the way they should.
That’s not subtle.

Just like the stomach digests and the eyes see—
different parts of the brain handle different jobs.

My main issue is in the prefrontal cortex—
the part responsible for control, focus, decisions.
Some other systems don’t coordinate properly either.

I was born like this.
I just found out late.

---

## [Glasses]

Imagine someone who’s been seeing blurry their whole life.

They don’t know it’s their eyes.
They think the world is just… like that.

Now imagine they see clearly for one moment—through someone else’s eyes.

That’s a shock.
“Oh… this is how everyone else sees?”

Then they learn glasses exist.
It’s fixable.

But they can’t access it.
And everyone around them says, “You’re fine.”

That’s still happening—just not with eyes.

---

## [Science]

In ADHD brains, dopamine is lower.

Dopamine is what drives motivation, impulse control,
and the ability to *start*.

Back in 1937, stimulants were given to hyperactive kids—
and they became calmer.

That flipped the assumption.

The issue isn’t “too much energy.”
It’s **poorly regulated energy.**

Later, we learned: these medications increase dopamine at the synapse.

---

## [Melatonin vs Dopamine]

You can’t force yourself to sleep.

Sleep happens when melatonin is released.

Same thing here—

You can’t force yourself to start work.

**You need dopamine.**

---

## [The Core Mechanism]

Neurotypical brains have a baseline level of dopamine—
enough to start tasks just because they “should.”

ADHD brains don’t.

Unless something is interesting, new, urgent, or meaningful—
the brain literally doesn’t produce the chemistry needed to begin.

Not metaphorically.

Biologically.

Dr. William Dodson calls this an “interest-based nervous system.”

This isn’t personality.
It’s chemistry.

---

## [What It Looks Like]

A few examples:

* I know what I need to do—but I can’t start
* I forget what I was saying mid-sentence
* Small distractions completely derail focus
* Boring tasks feel physically heavy
* Sometimes I disappear into something for hours (hyperfocus)
* Sleep doesn’t come easily—my brain won’t shut off

From the outside, this looks like laziness.

It’s not.

---

## [Two Networks]

There are two main modes:

* Task mode (TPN)
* Mind-wandering mode (DMN)

Normally, they alternate.

In ADHD, they overlap.

So even when I try to focus—
something else is running in the background.

---

## [“You’re Fine”]

When I shared my diagnosis, I heard:

“Don’t overthink.”
“You’re fine.”
“That’s not real.”

When people don’t understand something,
they label it as personality.

The brain writes its own user manual.
Even when there’s a bug, it calls it normal.

---

## [Inner Reality]

People say, “Undiagnosed people are doing fine.”

How do you know?

You’ve seen the surface. Not the inside.

A lot of us are masking—
burning energy just to look normal.

That mask drops in private.

---

## [Consequences]

ADHD itself isn’t dangerous.

**Untreated ADHD is.**

* Depression
* Anxiety
* Addiction (often self-medication)
* Relationship breakdowns
* Academic and job instability

These aren’t moral failures.
They’re predictable outcomes.

And in Nepal, it’s worse—
because many don’t even know this exists.

---

## [Why I’m Saying This]

I’m not saying this because it’s comfortable.

I can’t fix this alone.

This isn’t a solo problem.

I don’t want to keep explaining this every week.
I want treatment.

---

## [Policy — Direct]

Amphetamine and Methylphenidate
are standard ADHD treatments worldwide.

They’re not accessible here.

This is not a “Western idea.”
This is human biology.

Blocking treatment because of misuse—
is bad policy.

Regulate it.
But make it available.

---

## [End]

Let me live the rest of my life
with a functioning brain.

This isn’t luxury.
It’s treatment.
It’s a right.

Make it accessible.
